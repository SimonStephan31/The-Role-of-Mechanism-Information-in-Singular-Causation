3   Instruction

Please read the following fictitious scenario:

There once existed a mighty empire called Tristonia. Below you see a map of the empire that shows the most important locations. As you can see, there is the King’s Palace in the north of the empire. There are also two important fortresses in the south of Tristonia, called the Western Castle and the Eastern Castle.




Although Tristonia is a prosperous kingdom, the health care system is rather bad. The only healer of Tristonia works in the King’s palace. For this reason, whenever an emergency occurs in either the Western or the Eastern Castle, the castles must send an emergency call to the palace.

In this study, you will learn about Tristonia’s emergency-call system. First of all, we will show you on the next screen how the emergency-call system of Tristonia works. Please click “Continue” to learn about it.

4   Instruction_part2

Basic information about Tristonia's emergency-call system:

The emergency call system of Tristonia is based on three factors: Carrier Pigeons, Telegraphs, and Pony Riders. Both the Western and the Eastern Castle have a dovecote in one of their towers in which they keep carrier pigeons that can be used to send an emergency call to the Palace. The pigeons cannot fly the whole way to the King’s Palace, however. Both castles therefore send their pigeons to intermediate telegraph towers positioned half way to the palace (see illustration below). These intermediate telegraph towers have the task to pass emergency signals delivered by carrier pigeons from the castles on to the King's Palace.


Of course, it might happen from time to time that a carrier pigeon sent by a castle gets lost on its way to the intermediate station. Also, since telegraphy is a new technology based on electricity generated with wind energy, an intermediate telegraph tower may not always work correctly. When a tower has problems with its electricity supply, it cannot send out an emergency telegram to the place.


To handle situations in which received emergency calls cannot be passed on to the palace via telegraphy, there have been pony stations installed next to each intermediate telegraph tower. Whenever a telegraph tower is out of electricity and cannot pass on a received signal, a rider is sent out instead to trigger the emergency alarm in the palace. These situations are not optimal, however, because it is a long way to the palace and riders sometimes get attacked and murdered by bandits living in the mountains.



Please click “Continue” to learn more.

5   Instruction_part3

Specific facts about the emergency-call system:

You have already learned the most basic facts about how the emergency-call system of Tristonia works. On this page, we give you some additional information that is important to keep in mind.

Please note that:

  • The communication system works only in one direction, from the castles to the palace. This is illustrated in the picture below by the yellow arrows.

 

  • Each castle can only send their emergency carrier pigeons to their intermediate station (the ones lying on the direct path to the palace) and not to the stations of the other castle.

 

  • Whenever an emergency message successfully reaches the palace (either delivered via a telegram or by a pony rider), an automatic alarm bell is triggered in the palace.

  • Unfortunately, once triggered by an incoming message, this alarm bell of the palace has turned out to be extremely difficult to reset. Once activated, it keeps ringing the whole day.




We will next tell you what your task is going to be. Please click "Continue" to learn about your task.

For each day, you will see whether a castle sent out an emergency pigeon or not and whether an alarm occurred in the palace or not. You will also see what happened at the intermediate stations. An example is shown in the picture below.




As you can see, the example shows a day on which both the Eastern and the Western Castle sent out an emergency pigeon.

The intermediate tower of the Western Castle received the pigeon and then sent out a telegram to the palace.

The intermediate tower of the Eastern Castle also received the pigeon from its castle but apparently could not send out a telegram to the palace. To pass on the emergency call, the crew at the pony station therefore sent out a rider to the palace.

The icon with the ringing alarm bell above the palace indicates that an incoming emergency call successfully triggered an alarm on this day.

Below you see a list showing the icons for all the different events that can occur.




The first Icon signals that an emergency pigeon was sent out by a castle.

The second icon means that a telegram was sent out.

The “?” icon means that it is unknown what happened at this location on the given day.

The “X” icon indicates cases where a message was not received (due to a pigeon having gotten lost, technical issues of a telegraph, or because a sent out rider went missing).

The last icon, the “alarm” icon, indicates that an incoming signal successfully triggered the alarm bell in the King’s Palace.
