3   Instruction_part1

Please read the following fictitious scenario:

There once existed a mighty empire called Tristonia. Below you see a map of the empire that shows the most important locations. As you can see, there is the King’s Palace in the north of the empire. There are also two important fortresses in the south of Tristonia, called the Western Castle and the Eastern Castle.


Although Tristonia is a prosperous kingdom, the health care system is rather bad. The only healer of Tristonia works in the King’s Palace. For this reason, whenever an emergency occurs in either the Western or the Eastern Castle, the castles must send an emergency call to the palace.

In this study, you will learn about Tristonia’s emergency-call system. First of all, we will show you on the next screen how the emergency-call system of Tristonia works. Please click “Continue” to learn about it.

4   Instruction_part2

Basic information about Tristonia's emergency-call system:

The emergency call system of Tristonia is based on pony riders. Both the Western and the Eastern Castle have a pony station close by (see illustration below) from where emergency riders can be sent to the palace. The pony riders can’t go the whole way to the King’s Palace, however. Both castles therefore send their riders to intermediate pony stations (see illustration below). These intermediate pony stations keep fresh ponies in their stables and have fresh riders on post who can take over and pass on emergency signals delivered by the castles to the palace.

 

Please click “Continue” to learn more.

5   Instruction_part3

Specific facts about the emergency-call system:


You have already learned the most basic facts about how the emergency-call system of Tristonia works. On this page, we give you some additional information that is important to keep in mind.

 

  • The communication system works only in one direction, from the castles to the palace. This is illustrated in the picture  below by the yellow arrows.

 

  • Each castle can only send their emergency pony riders to their intermediate pony station (the ones to which the yellow arrows are pointing) and not to the pony station of the other castle.

 

  • Whenever an emergency message successfully reaches the palace, an automatic alarm bell is triggered in the palace.

 

  • Unfortunately, once triggered by an incoming message, the alarm bell of the palace has turned out to be extremely difficult to reset. Once activated, it keeps ringing the whole day. Luckily, there are never false alarms at least. That is, the probability that the alarm goes off in the palace if no castle sent out an emergency call is 0%.



We will next tell you what your task is going to be. Please click "Continue" to learn about your task.

6.1   Instruction_part3b

Unfortunately, sent out Pony Riders do not always make it to their destinations because they sometimes get attacked and murdered on their ways by evil robber barons who are living in the forests and mountains of Tristonia. The map below shows the high-danger regions (red) where the evil barons strike most often.

As you can see, the red high-danger zones are lying on the paths from the two intermediate stations to the King’s Palace. That is, pony riders sent out by the intermediate stations must fear that they get attacked on their way to the palace. There are two different and independent groups of robber barons: One group, the western group, concentrates its attacks on the western path. The other group, the eastern group, concentrates its attacks on the eastern path.

You can also see that the high-danger zones are restricted to the paths leading from the intermediate stations to the King’s Palace. That is, pony riders sent out from the castles are quite safe on their way to their intermediate station. These regions (green) are therefore called the low-danger zones.

 


Please click "Continue" to learn more.

6.2   Instruction_part3c

The royal statistician of Tristonia has collected reliable statistical data on the risk of attacks by the two groups of evil robber barons. This statistical information is given below. As you can see, there is a 90% chance in the red high-danger zones that a pony rider on his way from his intermediate station to the palace gets attacked and murdered by evil barons. That is, only 1 out of 10 riders sent out from their intermediate station eventually makes it to the King’s Palace. A very low success rate.

You can also see that the risk in the green zones is much lower. Here, the probability that a rider sent out from his castle to his intermediate station gets attacked and murdered by evil barons is only 10%. That is, 9 out of 10 riders sent out from their castle make it to their intermediate station. A very high success rate.
